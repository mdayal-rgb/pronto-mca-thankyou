Pronto Cares · Data Architecture

From food waste to safe, neighbor-first meal delivery

Challenge

Too much food is wasted while families still miss meals.

In the U.S., roughly 30 to 40 percent of the food supply goes unsold or uneaten, while many households still face food insecurity. Pronto Cares closes that gap with real-time rescue and delivery operations.

Source: USDA Food Loss and Waste estimates (U.S.).

Solution

Turn surplus into safe, on-time deliveries.

Pronto Cares coordinates donations, recipient need, and driver availability in one flow. Every handoff is safety-aware, time-aware, and optimized to reduce waste while increasing successful meal delivery.

Mission

Food as medicine. Do no harm.

Deliver meals with dignity, warmth, and trust. The mission is not just moving food — it is making sure the right meal reaches the right person, safely and on time.

Architecture

System of Record

Salesforce Org

The primary system of record for the Pronto Cares org — the home base every other layer plugs into.

Inputs: Agent interactions, service workflows, and CRM entities.

Transform: Operational context, routing rules, and case state are normalized.

Output: Structured records consumed by Data Cloud and downstream telemetry joins.

Telemetry

Snowflake

Zero-copy integration for live telemetry powering driver and order tracking for rescue dispatch.

Inputs: Driver pings, order lifecycle events, and dispatch timestamps.

Transform: Near real-time telemetry joins identify delay and exception patterns.

Output: Dispatch confidence signals used by Rescue Match & Dispatch.

Enrichment / External Data

Amazon S3

Enrichment data for matching donors and recipients — optimized for demo-friendly setup.

Inputs: Partner files, preferences, and recipient profile enrichments.

Transform: Batch validation and shape alignment for harmonization.

Output: Enriched records merged into Data Cloud DMOs for matching quality.

Unified Data Layer

Data Cloud DMOs

A connected hub that lets Rescue Match and Dispatch reason across recipients, donors, items, and drivers.

  • Recipients
  • Donors
  • Rescued Items
  • Dispatches
  • CRM contact + address + phone

Inputs: CRM records, Snowflake telemetry, and S3 enrichment feeds.

Transform: Identity resolution and canonical DMO mapping across domains.

Output: A single reasoning graph consumed by agents and analytics.

Visualization / Analytics

Tableau (or other analytics tools)

Tableau Next advantage: a single connector makes all data available through a semantic layer for AI-ready insights.

Inputs: Unified DMO tables and operational outcome metrics.

Transform: Semantic-layer modeling for role-specific dashboards.

Output: Explainable performance views for mission, safety, and delivery SLAs.

RAG / Policy Grounding

Grounds the Safe-to-Eat Guardian in real policy, always with citation-backed answers.

Primary

Einstein Data Library (ADL)

Food safety policy documents power grounded responses for the Safe-to-Eat Guardian.

Inputs: Food-safety policies and grounded governance documents.

Transform: Retrieval + citation selection for policy-safe generation.

Output: Traceable answers for Safe-to-Eat Guardian decisions.

deterministic fallback

Fallback

Custom Metadata Type

Pronto_Policy__mdt

Deterministic citation store for decision codes, deployed with the app independently of indexing pipelines.

Inputs: Curated policy snippets keyed by decision reason code.

Transform: Deterministic lookup when ADL retrieval is unavailable.

Output: Guaranteed citation fallback for resilient trust behavior.
